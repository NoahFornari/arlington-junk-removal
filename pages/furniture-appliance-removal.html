Furniture & Appliance Removal in Arlington, TX

Arlington Junk Removal picks up and disposes of old furniture and appliances from homes and businesses across Arlington and the DFW area. We handle all the heavy lifting — you just point at what needs to go.

Furniture we remove

Getting rid of large furniture is one of the most common reasons Arlington homeowners call us. City trash pickup won't take bulky items, and hauling a couch to the dump yourself requires a truck and a lot of effort. We take care of it all.

We remove sofas, couches, sectionals, loveseats, recliners, dining tables, dining chairs, desks, office chairs, bookshelves, entertainment centers, dressers, nightstands, bed frames, headboards, mattresses, box springs, futons, patio furniture, and outdoor tables and chairs. If two people can lift it, we can haul it.

Appliances we remove

Old appliances are heavy, awkward, and sometimes require special disposal. We handle all of that for you. We remove refrigerators, freezers, washers, dryers, dishwashers, stoves, ovens, microwaves, window AC units, water heaters, and dehumidifiers. We recommend having gas appliances disconnected by a professional before we arrive.

How it works

Call or text us at (817) 705-6612 with a description or photo of the items you need removed. We'll give you a ballpark price right away. When we arrive, we confirm the final price on-site before we start. Then we carry everything out, load it up, sweep the area, and take it all away. Most single-item or small furniture jobs take under an hour.

Pricing

Single items like a couch, mattress, or refrigerator typically fall in the quarter-load range of $150–$250. Multiple rooms of furniture or several large appliances scale up by volume — a half load runs $250–$400, and larger jobs go from there. We always quote before we start and never add hidden fees.

What happens to your old furniture and appliances

We don't just dump everything in a landfill. Usable furniture in decent condition gets donated to local Arlington charities and churches. Working appliances are donated or taken to appliance recyclers. Metal components from broken appliances are recycled as scrap. Our goal is to divert as much as possible from landfills.
